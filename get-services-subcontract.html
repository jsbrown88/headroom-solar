1.Purpose & structure

  1. 1.1 Headroom wins, designs and manages commercial solar projects. Get-Services delivers the physical installation as Headroom's subcontractor.
  2. 1.2 The relationship is non-exclusive and project-by-project: each project is instructed under an agreed works order incorporating these terms. Confirm whether Get-Services seeks any minimum-volume or preferred-installer commitment.
  3. 1.3 Get-Services is an independent contractor. Nothing creates a partnership, joint venture or employment between the parties.

2.Scope of works — install labour only

Per project works order, Get-Services provides installation labour and access only: site set-up, scaffolding/access, mechanical and electrical installation of Headroom-supplied hardware, commissioning, testing and handover documentation, to Headroom's approved design and specification.

  1. 2.1 Headroom procures and supplies the hardware. Headroom buys the panels, inverters, mounting and balance-of-system, pays for them, and free-issues them to site for installation. Get-Services does not procure equipment.
  2. 2.2 Headroom retains and owns the end-to-end client engagement: origination, survey, design, proposal, DNO application and the client contract. Andy Hollis (Headroom) manages the client relationship and attends site for day one of installation. Get-Services does not design, does not contract with the client, and does not direct the client relationship.

2A.Free-issue hardware — title, risk & care

  1. 2A.1 All hardware supplied by Headroom remains Headroom's property at all times; title does not pass to Get-Services.
  2. 2A.2 From the point hardware is delivered to or received on site, Get-Services takes care, custody and control of it and is responsible for its safe storage, handling and protection until installed and signed off.
  3. 2A.3 Get-Services is liable for loss of, or damage to, Headroom's hardware caused by its acts, omissions, negligence or poor storage, and must insure for it (see clause 9).
  4. 2A.4 Manufacturer warranties on the hardware run to Headroom / the client directly, not to Get-Services.

3.Accreditations & competence (to verify before first works order)

  • MCS accreditation (and/or relevant scheme for system sizes), with current certificate provided.
  • Electrical competence: NICEIC / NAPIT registration; BS 7671 qualified operatives.
  • Working-at-height, scaffolding and CDM competence; current method statements and risk assessments (RAMS).
  • Evidence the above covers Headroom's target system band (50kW–5MW). If Get-Services' accreditation caps below this, define the ceiling.

4.Health, safety & CDM

  1. 4.1 Get-Services acts as principal contractor under CDM 2015 for the installation works, carrying the associated duties (construction phase plan, site safety, notifications). Confirm Get-Services accepts the principal contractor role — this is the single most important risk allocation in the document.
  2. 4.2 Get-Services complies with all applicable health, safety, building and electrical regulations and standards.

5.Pricing — install labour only

  1. 5.1 Get-Services is priced for installation labour and access only, excluding hardware (which Headroom procures). Proposed basis: a fixed install price per project, or a labour rate per kWp installed / day-rate by crew. Agree the install-only basis; this is a smaller number than a full EPC price because the kit is free-issued.
  2. 5.2 Headroom contracts the client and prices the whole job (hardware + install + design + management). The Get-Services install price is confidential and not disclosed to the client.
  3. 5.3 Variations only by written instruction with an agreed price before work proceeds.

6.Payment & cashflow

  1. 6.1 Stage payments aligned to milestones (e.g. mobilisation, materials on site, mechanical complete, commissioning/handover). [Define stages and %.]
  2. 6.2 Payment terms [X] days from valid invoice. Match Get-Services' payment milestones to Headroom's client receipts to avoid a working-capital gap.
  3. 6.3 Retention of [5%] held to the end of the defects period (clause 7).

7.Quality, standards, warranties & defects

  • Works to Headroom's approved design, manufacturer instructions and BS 7671; equipment to agreed specification (IEC-certified panels/inverters).
  • Workmanship warranty of [period]; defects-liability period of [12 months] from handover, with Get-Services rectifying defective work at its cost.
  • Full pass-through of manufacturer product warranties to Headroom / the client.
  • Headroom holds a QA sign-off gate before the project is accepted and the client signed off (journey stage 8).

8.Non-circumvention & non-solicitation (binding on signature)

This is the clause that protects the model. Headroom hands Get-Services direct access to its clients at the site visit and installation. Without this protection, the installer can take the relationship.
  1. 8.1 Get-Services will not, during the relationship and for [24] months after, directly or indirectly solicit, contract with or supply solar or related services to any client, prospect or introduced contact of Headroom, except through Headroom.
  2. 8.2 This covers clients met, surveyed or installed for via Headroom, and any contact in Headroom's pipeline disclosed to Get-Services.
  3. 8.3 Breach entitles Headroom to a referral remedy of [15%] of the circumvented contract value, without prejudice to other remedies. Mirror the BridgePoint–HH non-solicit remedy.
  4. 8.4 Mutual non-solicitation of each other's staff for the same period.

9.Insurance & liability

  1. 9.1 Get-Services maintains, and evidences: public liability [£5m], products liability [£5m], employers' liability [£10m] and contractors' all-risk cover appropriate to project value, including cover for Headroom's free-issued hardware while in Get-Services' care, custody and control (clause 2A). Verify limits before first works order. Headroom should also confirm its own goods/contractors'-all-risk position on hardware it owns on site.
  2. 9.2 Get-Services indemnifies Headroom against claims arising from the works, its negligence, defective workmanship or health-and-safety breaches, and names Headroom's interest where appropriate.
  3. 9.3 Liability caps, exclusions and the position on consequential loss to be set in the definitive subcontract.

10.Client data & IP

All client data, contacts, designs, monitoring data and the client relationship belong to Headroom. Get-Services may use project data only to deliver the works and may not retain or reuse client contact data. The CRM and monitoring stack sits with Headroom, not the installer.

11.Confidentiality

Each party keeps the other's confidential information, pricing and client information confidential. Binds on signature of these heads.

12.Programme & capacity

Get-Services to confirm typical lead times, current capacity and its ability to scale with Headroom's pipeline. Each works order sets a programme with reasonable-endeavours dates and a process for client-caused delay. Headroom will line up a second installer to de-risk capacity.

13.Term & termination

Master terms run for [period], renewing by agreement; either party may terminate on [notice], or immediately for material breach or insolvency. Live works orders complete or transfer on termination. Clauses 8, 10 and 11 survive.

14.Costs, status & law

  1. 14.1 Each party bears its own costs of negotiating the definitive subcontract.
  2. 14.2 These heads are subject to contract and not binding except clauses 8, 11 and 14.
  3. 14.3 Governed by the law of England and Wales.

15.Information needed from Get-Services

  • Full legal name, company number, registered office.
  • Copies of accreditations (MCS, NICEIC/NAPIT) and insurance schedules with limits.
  • Confirmation it will act as CDM principal contractor.
  • Pricing basis (fixed per project / rate per kWp) and indicative rates.
  • Typical lead times, current and scalable capacity, and geographic coverage.
  • Standard warranty and defects terms.
