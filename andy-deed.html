1.Background

  1. 1.1 Headroom is a commercial solar venture operated as a ring-fenced division and trading brand of BridgePoint ("the Division"). It is not, at the date of these heads, a separate legal entity.
  2. 1.2 The parties wish to build the Division together, sharing its economics equally, with a clear path to a jointly owned company once revenue justifies it.
  3. 1.3 The agreed shape is "economic now, equity later": an equal share of the Division's profit from the outset, plus an option for Andy to convert to a 50% shareholding in a future company.

2.The Division & ring-fence

  1. 2.1 BridgePoint will operate the Division through a separate cost-and-revenue ledger (cost-centre coding and/or a bank sub-account) so its economics can be identified distinctly from BridgePoint's other activities.
  2. 2.2 "Division Net Profit" means the Division's gross revenue less its directly attributable costs (cost of works, sales, marketing, design, DNO, finance and a fair, agreed allocation of shared overhead), calculated by BridgePoint and reviewed by its accountant. Confirm the overhead-allocation basis.

3.Profit share

  1. 3.1 Division Net Profit is shared 50% to Andy and 50% to BridgePoint, subject to vesting (clause 5).
  2. 3.2 The BridgePoint 50% is then allocated within BridgePoint among its principals (Mark Evans-Smith, Virginia Filmer-Sankey, Kevin [surname] and others) under a separate internal arrangement to which Andy is not a party. Andy negotiates with one counterparty, not four.
  3. 3.3 Profit is calculated and distributed quarterly, after retention of agreed working capital to fund the Division's pipeline.

5.Vesting

  1. 5.1 Andy's 50% profit share vests as follows: 25% on signature of the definitive deed; the remaining 75% over [24] months of continued involvement, or against agreed pipeline / revenue milestones.
  2. 5.2 Unvested share does not entitle Andy to that portion of profit until vested. Protects the venture if Andy departs early.

6.Equity conversion option

  1. 6.1 Andy holds a right, exercisable on incorporation of a dedicated company ("Headroom Energy Ltd" or similar), to subscribe for 50% of its shares on terms economically equivalent to this deed.
  2. 6.2 Incorporation is triggered by mutual agreement or when Division revenue reaches [threshold]. On conversion, the vested profit-share position carries across; unvested portions continue to vest on the same schedule as equity.
  3. 6.3 The new company's governance follows clause 7 unless the parties agree otherwise.

7.Governance & decision rights

Equal economics do not require equal control on every matter. Proposed division of authority:

  • Andy leads: delivery, technical design, operations, installer management, day-to-day running.
  • BridgePoint leads: finance, brand, marketing, capital, legal and contracting.
  • Joint / reserved matters (both agree): annual budget, pricing policy, taking on debt, incorporation, hiring senior roles, any commitment over [£value], admitting new partners.
A deadlock mechanism should be agreed (for example, escalation then independent expert) so a 50/50 split does not freeze the business.

8.Intellectual property

  1. 8.1 The "Headroom" name, marks, domain and intelligence engine remain owned by BridgePoint and are licensed to the Division (and to any future Headroom Energy Ltd) for as long as this arrangement subsists.
  2. 8.2 IP, processes and materials created specifically for the Division belong to the Division / future company. Andy assigns to the Division any such IP he creates in the course of the venture.

9.Restrictive covenants

  • Mutual non-compete in UK commercial solar for the duration and [12] months after, save for Andy's pre-existing interests disclosed and listed in a schedule.
  • Non-solicitation of the Division's clients, pipeline, staff and installer partners for [12] months after exit.

10.Good leaver / bad leaver

  1. 10.1 Good leaver (ill health, death, removal without cause): retains vested share; unvested share lapses.
  2. 10.2 Bad leaver (material breach, dishonesty, breach of covenant): vested and unvested share may be reduced or forfeited as the definitive deed specifies.

11.Confidentiality

Each party keeps the other's confidential information and the terms of this arrangement confidential. This clause binds on signature of these heads.

12.Term & termination

The arrangement continues until replaced by the company structure in clause 6 or terminated by mutual agreement, by notice of [period], or for material breach. Exit settles vested profit share to the leaving date.

13.Costs, status & law

  1. 13.1 Each party bears its own costs of negotiating the definitive deed.
  2. 13.2 These heads are subject to contract and not legally binding except clauses 11 and 13.
  3. 13.3 Governed by the law of England and Wales.
